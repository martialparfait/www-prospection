## 3. Personal data we collect

### 3.1. Data collected through our website

When you visit https://www.world-wellness-weekend.org/ or https://map.world-wellness-weekend.org/ and when you register your establishment as a participant:

- Establishment identifiers: name, address, city, country, postal code
- Professional contact details: phone number, email address, website
- Identity of the registering responsible person: first name, last name, role, professional email
- Technical data: IP address, browser type, pages visited (via cookies — see Section 10)

### 3.2. Data collected for outreach to wellness establishments

To invite wellness establishments — including fitness clubs, yoga studios, Pilates studios, hotels with sport and spa facilities, martial arts dojos, running clubs and similar venues — to participate in the World Wellness Weekend, we collect:

- Establishment name, address, city, country, postal code
- Activity category (yoga, fitness, spa, hospitality, etc.)
- Professional contact information: email, phone number, website
- Identity and role of the operational responsible person (owner, manager, general manager) when this information is publicly disclosed by the establishment
- Contextual data relevant to the invitation (amenities offered, languages spoken, opening hours)

**No sensitive data** within the meaning of Article 9 of the GDPR (health, political opinions, religious beliefs, etc.) is collected.

## 4. Sources of data

In accordance with **Article 14 of the GDPR**, we inform you that data relating to establishments and their managers is collected from the following sources:

- **Directly from you** when you register on our website or contact us
- **From publicly accessible sources**, including:
  - The official website of your establishment (Contact, About, Team, Legal Notice pages)
  - **OpenStreetMap** (ODbL license)
  - **Overture Maps Foundation** (CDLA Permissive 2.0 / Apache 2.0 licenses)

- **From GDPR-compliant B2B data enrichment providers** (notably Dropcontact for France and Belgium, whose operations are independently audited)

- **Right of access** (Art. 15) — to obtain a copy of the data we hold about you
- **Right to rectification** (Art. 16) — to correct inaccurate data
- **Right to erasure** ("right to be forgotten", Art. 17) — subject to our legal obligations
